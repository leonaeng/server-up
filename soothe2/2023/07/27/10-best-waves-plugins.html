About Waves

Waves is a company that has been at the forefront of music production plugins and software for quite some time. In fact, some of the very first Waves plugins began coming out more than three decades ago!

The beauty of Waves is that the company covers just about any kind of music processing you could imagine. From emulations of classic outboard gear to modern, digital production tools for surgical work, we cant think of a plugin that Waves is missing. 

Its almost overwhelming! 

So without further ado, lets go through a few processing categories and explore the best Waves plugins in each. By the way, if you’re a big Waves fan, be sure to check out my 14 favorite Waves vocal plugins. I especially appreciate time saving plugins like de-breath and vocal rider!

Best EQ Waves Plugin – Scheps 73

You probably know that were massive fans of vintage hardware emulations if youve ever read any of the other articles on the site. And  the Scheps 73 by Waves is a beautiful emulation of one of the most iconic pieces of analog hardware around, the Neve 1073.

The Neve 1073, for those who dont know, is a legendary microphone preamp and equalizer. It was and is famous for its fixed frequency band EQ section, which provides a musical sound and buttery saturation that is difficult to capture anywhere else. It sounds delicious on vocal tracks, drums, and all variety of acoustic instruments. 

One great thing about the EQ section is that it is incredibly straightforward. You only get a single high-pass filter and three frequency bands, all of which were chosen for their utility. The limitations here force you to use your ears to get the sound youre after, rather than using a fancy, real-time visualizer to make your decisions. Plus, you can boost frequencies at normally outlandish levels without it ever sounding harsh or unnatural. That alone is probably the main selling point of Scheps 73. 

I often like to use the Scheps 73 as the main EQ in my mixing chain for every instrument, as it gives every instrument a similar, analog vibe, while I treat the mix as if it were coming through an analog console. But if the Scheps 73 isn’t what you’re looking for, though, check out my review of the best EQ VSTs. It was developed with the help of mix engineer Andrew Scheps, who is famous for working with artists like the Red Hot Chili Peppers and Adele.

Pros of the Waves Scheps 73 EQ

  • Simple & straightforward
  • Very musical
  • Warm analog sound

 

Cons of the Waves Scheps 73 EQ

  • Limited control

Best Waves Channel Strip – SSL EV2

While I think UAD makes one of the best SSL 4000E emulations, the barrier to entry is quite high. The UAD SSL 4000E plugin is wildly expensive and requires the user has UAD hardware to run it.

So, for the majority of producers, its not the best option.

Luckily, Waves has an SSL 4000E emulation that is right alongside UADs in terms of sound and functionality. The value is much better, and the sound is terrific. Im talking about the SSL EV2. Waves recently renewed their original emulation of the SSL 4000E, and its incredible!

Youll find rich, harmonic saturation, two options for EQ curves (including the Brown and Black versions found in the original hardware), and a mic mode, giving users the option to introduce more extreme gain to their signals. Of course, you also get that fat SSL compression that youre used to! There are also high-pass and low-pass filters for removing high and lower frequencies. 

There are over 600 presets to choose from, which come from some of the best producers and mix engineers in the industry. Another thing I love about the Waves SSL EV2 is that the plugin is also very easy on your CPU, meaning you can insert it on just about every channel in your mix to treat everything as if it were running through a console, unlike the UAD emulation. 

Pros of the Waves SSL EV2 Channel Strip

  • Lightweight
  • Incredible sound and control
  • Efficient all-in-one channel strip design

Cons of the Waves SSL EV2 Channel Strip

  • None

If the Waves SSL EV2 doesn’t seem like the right choice for you, check out this review of the best channel strip plugins that includes channel strips from all designers.

Best Waves Saturation Plugin – Cobalt Saphira

There are many types of saturation plugins on the market, though none of them is quite as versatile as Cobalt Saphira. This plugin delivers an unreal level of control and musicality, perfect for those who want an advanced harmonic-shaping tool that can give their tracks glue, depth, and warmth. Plus, it works on just about any type of instrument or vocal you pair it with! Just a little bit of saturation can go a long way!

The one thing that separates this plugin from many other saturation plugins is the level of unprecedented digital control you get. While it can certainly be nice to have saturation plugins that emulate the inconsistencies and quirks of tapes or tubes, having the ability to balance even and odd harmonics, choose between different harmonic modes, or pick from five tape speeds to add flutter and wow modulation to your tracks is awesome. 

Another unique thing about Cobalt Saphira is that it allows you to control your harmonics with or without compression. You can get all of the harmonic goodness that you get from your standard saturation plugin without compressing it to death. If you only want that slight bit of glue to make your mix sound cohesive without squashing the transients, Saphira is an excellent choice!

The only thing I’d say is experiment a lot with this plugin, because it has huge range of subtle tones that you can use to bring your mix to life!

Pros of the Waves Cobalt Saphira Saturation Plugin

  • Precise control
  • Uncompressed sound
  • Powerful Interface

 

Cons of the Waves Cobalt Saphira Saturation Plugin

  • Interface is a little complicated
  • No fun surprises 

If the Cobalt Saphira doesn’t seem like the right choice for you, be sure to check out this comprehensive review of the best saturation and distortion plugins.

Best Waves Plugin for Drums – Eddie Kramer Drum Channel

Eddie Kramer is a legend of the mixing world for having some of the best drum tones in the industry, which is why were pretty lucky that he teamed up with Waves to develop a plugin that captures the essence of his tones. Whether you want that dry, pillowy sound famous in 70s rock and alternative, massive Zeppelin-style sounds with atmosphere for days, or the squashed, heavily-compressed sounds of 60s rock and roll, the Eddie Kramer Drum Channel can get you where you need to go.

The beauty of this plugin lies in its simplicity. There are tons of presets to get you started, and individual modes for different drum channels, including bass drum, toms, overheads, snare, hi-hats, and room. 

I love the fact that you only get treble and bass controls on the EQ section and compression and gate control on the dynamics section. Theres no need to deal with frequency numbers of compression parameters. Its all about listening to the sound to see what fits best. 

While I typically like to use this plugin on individual drums, and it sounds great on kick drums, snares and overheads, it also works as a drum bus plugin to quickly dial in great tones for your entire kit. If you need some tips dialing in your tones more precisely, check out this guide on how to EQ drums.

Pros of the Eddie Kramer Drum Channel

  • Dead simple
  • Good sound

 

Cons of the Eddie Kramer Drum Channel

  • Too simple?

Best Waves Reverb – H-Reverb

There are so many types of reverb plugins you can choose from, including plate reverb, chamber reverb, room reverb, and spring reverb (here is a guide to the 7 Types of Reverb and When to Use Them). The beauty of H-Reverb from Waves is that it takes all of the best reverb sounds and sticks them into an extremely customizable piece of software for all your space-creating needs. Given its customization, it’s a good idea to take sometime to familiarize yourself with all its features by experimenting with its presets.

H-Reverb might just be one of the most versatile reverb plugins on the market altogether. Waves was able to combine the best concepts of classic reverb hardware with more advanced concepts found in the world of digital processing. As a result, you get one of the most flexible and high-end-sounding reverb plugins around.  

If youre curious, H-Reverb uses Finite Impulse Response Technology, providing rich, deep, and realistic decay times that can sit well in just about any mix. You can start by pulling up one of the hundreds of available presets and start tweaking to get the sound you need.  

The plugin comes with an EQ section, a modulation section, a dynamics section, and a timing section that allows you to adjust the decay envelopes of the reverb. Its basically every type of processing you may add to your reverb, built into a single piece of software.

Pros of the Waves H-Reverb

  • Amazing sound
  • Powerful control

Cons of the Waves H-Reverb 

  • Overwhelming options

Best Waves Plugin Bundle – Abbey Road Collection

Buying plugin bundles is a wonderful way to save money and gain access to a realm of plugins that fit together in a single unit. When Waves came out with the Abbey Road Collection, it made sonic waves in the industry. 

Abbey Road Studios is arguably the most famous studio on Earth. Its where The Beatles, Pink Floyd, Radiohead, and many more, recorded some of the best albums of all time. This unique bundle, which was developed in association with Abbey Road Studios, has a lineup of top-notch plugins that were meticulously modeled after some of the gear found throughout the studios iconic rooms. 

Some of the plugins in the Abbey Road collection include:

  • the TG1234 consoles,
  • Abbey Road TG Mastering Chain,
  • the REDD consoles,
  • the RS56 Passive EQ,
  • the J37 Tape Machine,
  • the Kings Microphone,
  • and the Reel ADT machine.

You also get access to some pretty incredible time-based reverb plugins, including the Abbey Road Chambers plugin and the Abbey Road Plates plugin. 

Whether youre making old-school tunes that require an analog feel or new-school tunes that could benefit from a touch of classic tone, the Abbey Road Collection is unmated in terms of versatility and tonality. 

Pros of the Abbey Road Bundle

  • Incredible value
  • Instant analog emulation
  • Wide range of plugins

 

Cons of the Abbey Road Bundle

  • None

Best Waves Limiter – L2

The L2 is one of the companys most iconic plugins and is probably why Waves made a name for itself in the world of limiting plugins so long ago. All of the plugins in the L Seris are known for their ease of use and punchy qualities. The L2 is no exception.

One thing I love about the L2 is that it has an intuitive interface that allows you to dial in the perfect amount of limiting in a matter of seconds. There are noise shaping and dithering controls built into the plugin as well, giving users more control over the final output. To help keep limiting as smooth as possible, Waves added Automatic Release Control to the plugin. 

Yes, the L2 is certainly not the most transparent limiter on the market today, but the unique sound that it provides can help deliver a punch to gutless mixes, no matter what genre you work in. 

One other feature present on theL2 Limiteris the Waves IDR (Increased Digital Resolution) technology. This unique technology preserves the resolution of your mix, even with heavy limiting.

If youre in the market for an inexpensive, easy-to-use limiter that can bring your mixes to life, the L2 is what youre after. If you’re looking for a more modern sounding limiter, here are the 7 best limiter plugins around.

 

Pros of the Waves L2 Limiter

  • Easy to use
  • Punchy

 

Cons of the Waves L2 Limiter

  • Limited controls
  • Not very transparent

Best Waves Compressor – API2500

API is a company that long ago created one of the most iconic VCA compressors around. The API 2500 from Waves emulates the original sound of this classic hardware rack compressor down to a T. As per usual, Waves did an excellent job at emulating the power and versatility of this hardware compressor at reasonable prices, which is perfect for buss compression on a variety of instruments. 

One unique thing about the API 2500 is that it uses a dual-channel design, meaning it can act on stereo single or two separate mono signals. The left and right channel have their own VU meter for monitoring and is controlled with easy-to-use compression settings. 

You get all of the standard functionality you could ask for in a compressor, including seven different ratio options, an adjustable threshold between -20dB and +10dB, and variable attack and release times. There is even an auto-makeup gain button, which helps maintain steady output levels as you adjust the attack and release settings, which is extremely helpful.  

Beyond that, this VCA compressor can be switched between a modern, feed-forward compression circuit and a vintage feedback compression circuit, depending on the sound youre after. It even has a handy Thrust control, which allows you to reduce low-end frequency compression to maintain punch and power while controlling high-end frequencies.

Itd be so difficult to fit everything great about this compressor into a single article, meaning its one you simply need to try for yourself. This is truly one of the best compressor plugins around and is the secret weapon in many producers arsenals, which is why you’ll find it in my round up of the best compressor VSTs.

 

Pros of the Waves API 2500 Compressor

  • Amazing sound
  • Soooo punchy
  • Versatile 

 

Cons of the Waves API 2500 Compressor

  • More complicated than others

Best Waves Tape Plugin – J37

The J37 is a tape saturation plugin made in conjunction with the team at Abbey Road Studios. Its a unique, virtual recreation of the iconic Studer Tape Machine that the studio built back in 1965. This particular tape machine was used to record many artists during that time, though the Beatles was arguably the most popular act. 

You can find my full review of Waves’ J-37 here.

When I’m going for a retro vibe, I like to load an instance of the J37 on all my individual tracks and dial in the input signal on each track for just the right amount of saturation.

Youre not going to find many fine-tuning controls on the J37, which we suppose is the beauty of it. However, you will find many unique modification controls, including Satin, Bias, Flutter, Wow, and Noise Level.You can choose thetape speed as well, which affects the overall fidelity and “weight” of the sound.

Tape delay is available as well, which you can use if youre looking for a warmer, mellower delay sound for your tracks. From ping-pong to feedback to slap delay models, you can pick from several unique delay circuits, each with its own unique flavor, and can use this as a standalone tape delay plugin.  

Beyond all of that, you can test out three tape formulas, including the 888, the 811, and the 815. They each have unique characteristics of the era, great for dialing in warm, modern, or edgy sounds.  

To apply this plugin effectively, you need a steady hand and some good judgment. However, if youre looking to impart a bit of retro flavor on your mixes, the J37 is one of the best plugins around.

Pros of the J37

  • Great analog sound
  • Add warmth and saturation

 

Cons of the J37

  • Need good judgment

Best Waves Plugin for Guitars – Chris Lord-Alge Guitars

Chris Lord Alge is undoubtedly the king of modern rock and alternative mixing, which is why it makes sense that he has a killer plugin for guitars. Even if you dont mix rock or metal music, this guitar plugin can help you dial in some of the most recognizable tones of the past few decades. 

There arent often plugins that deliver a level of instant gratification like CLA Guitars thanks to its straightforward user interface. You can find the full CLA Guitars review here.

Whether you need a clean country tone with sparkling reverb or a deeply-distorted heavy metal chug tone, this plugin can provide. There are three modes that start the signal chain off, including Clean, Crunch, and Heavy. There are DI and re-amping modes for each of these modes as well, meaning right off the bat, you can dial-in killer tones without messing with any other controls. 

Once you get your initial sound, you can begin shaping your tone even further using bass, treble, compression, delay, reverb, and doubling.

Id be lying if I knew what was going on under the hood of this plugin. However, what I do know is that CLA Guitars does something quite magical.

 

Pros of CLA Guitars

  • Dead simple
  • Great tone
  • Fast

 

Cons of CLA Guitars

  • No fine tuning

Best Waves Pitch Plugin – Waves Tune Real-Time

Melodyne and Antares make some of the best tuning plugins on the market, hands-down. Of course, to get the most out of those plugins, you have to do a lot of work – and pay a lot of money. Sometimes, its nice to let the robots do the work for you, which is pretty much the selling point of Waves Tune Real-Time. Waves Tune Real-Time is the “go to” plugin for live performance vocal tuning, thanks to its low latency and CPU load.

This plugin provides smooth and natural-sounding automatic pitch correction. Simply set the key and range of the song before dialing in the response parameters to get automatic tuning that is surprisingly transparent. Of course, you can always dial the Speed and Note Transition knobs all the way to zero for that T-Pain sound as well!

Want to find out more about Waves Tune Real Time?Here’s my full review!

Whether you need a live pitch correction or quick studio pitch correction, Waves Tune Real-Time is a great choice!

Pros of Waves Tune Real-Time

  • Easy
  • Fairly transparent
  • Affordable

 

Cons of Waves Tune Real-Time

  • Not as powerful as its peers

From a Frustrated Producer in a Ragtag Bedroom Studio to Major Placements on TV Earning $1,000s!

My name is Evan, and I’ve been making music since around 3rd grade. I’m from San Diego, California, but I’ve lived in Washington, DC for the last 20 years.

After 3 grueling years of grad school, though I had put aside serious attempts at making music. I found myself spending my days doing work that was dreadfully uncreative, with a ton of student student loan debt.

Which made me feel like my favorite parts of myself were withering.

But I didn’t know what to do about it.

Being in my early 30s with tons of student loan debt, in a world where there is “no money in music,” I felt like my youthful dreams of trying to “make it big” were dead. Like my music would remain unheard in my head and hard drive. 

Frustrated by my inability to get my music heard, I started researching solutions.

Instead, I wanted to find a way where I could focus on making the music and let someone else deal with promoting it. 

I realized the music licensing was the perfect opportunity for a solo artist like me to get my music heard, without having to do any promotion. I just need to focus on improving what I could control – my songwriting and my production skills.

While I still have a full-time day job, I have created systems that have allowed me to produce dozens of songs a year in my spare time.

Mysongs have been on Netflix, TV shows like the 90 Day Fiance, an award-winning indie film, and NPRs All Thing Considered. They’ve also been streamed millions of times.
