Oeksound Soothe2 Review

Mathew Vaughan
April 15, 2023 • Plugins

At last, here's my assessment of Soothe2. The landscape of music production has undergone substantial evolution over the recent decades. Transitioning from analog recording to the digital age, producers and engineers have continuously explored innovative avenues to enhance the caliber of their mixes. Within any mix, the management of dynamic range stands as a pivotal factor. Navigating dynamic range can pose difficulties, particularly when faced with intricate audio elements like vocals, drums, or guitars that may trigger undesired resonances. Enter Oeksound's Soothe2, addressing precisely this challenge.

Read time ~7 minutes

What Does Soothe2 Actually Do?

Oeksound Soothe2 is a dynamic equalizer designed to suppress unwanted resonances in audio material. It works by analyzing the audio signal and applying dynamic EQ processing to reduce or remove resonances in real-time. Resonances can cause listening fatigue, harshness, and other unpleasant artifacts in audio recordings, especially in complex material like vocals, drums, and guitars.

Connect, innovate, succeed

Soothe2 is a dynamic resonance suppressor that automatically targets resonant frequencies and can reduce them pretty much to extinction if you wish. Unlike SPIFF, which can both cut and boost transients, Soothe2 (and its predecessor SOOTHE) only reduces target signals, and the signals targeted are resonances rather than transients. So the two programs really complement each other.

Soothe2 can detect problematic resonances on the fly and apply appropriate reduction automatically. It can do so with little or no affect on the timbre of the sound. Soothe2 is oeksound’s second generation resonance correction tool and extends the frequency range that can be controlled right down to 20 Hz (SOOTHE worked only for mid and high frequency resonances, did not have a mid/side mode or the external side-chain input).

Tame vocal harshness, sibilance, or proximity

Undesirable resonances encountered in vocal recording can stem from a variety of sources: the vocal tone itself, the microphone used, its positioning, the acoustics of the room, and more. Soothe steps in to harmonize these resonances, resulting in a more subdued vocal tone, enhancing microphone output, achieving optimal proximity effect, and minimizing the impact of the room's reverberation. Rather than manually notching out frequencies from specific syllables or adjusting gain for each "ess" sound, Soothe automates this process for you, affording you the freedom to concentrate on the musical elements.

Features

Intuitive enough for beginners

Rather than investing minutes in painstakingly notching out resonances, incorporate Soothe to expedite the process and promptly delve into selecting which resonances to mitigate and to what extent. Employ the hard mode when faced with intricate challenges and the soft mode for a more subtle adjustment. The attack and release parameters permit you to modulate the processing pace, thereby enabling you to leverage Soothe as a comprehensive tonal equalizer.

What I found

Unless I was targeting a specific issue where one of the 100-plus presets could prove helpful, I discovered that commencing with a neutral "EQ" setting (remember, it's not actual EQ adjustment but control sensitivity) worked best for identifying the resonances I aimed to mitigate. Initially, I would increase the depth setting (sometimes opting for the hard mode), and engage delta mode to exclusively perceive/hear the resonances. By looping the problematic audio section, I swiftly adjusted sharpness, selectivity, attack, and release parameters to singularly accentuate the troublesome elements, essentially amplifying their prominence. Upon deactivating delta mode, I could then reduce the depth as necessary to achieve a natural-sounding correction.

The frequency curves are alterable through a pop-up window that allows for band type, frequency, sensitivity (the elevation of the band at the designated frequency, which, as I mentioned, does not function like a conventional EQ control), and appropriate Q adjustment. Alternatively, you can adjust the curves by manipulating nodes and employ the mouse wheel to modify Q values. Worth noting is the listen button positioned at the lower left, facilitating the monitoring of the delta signal within the selected frequency range—a valuable aid for precision targeting of problematic zones. The user manual provides comprehensive insight into all these functionalities and more.

Why Does Soothe2 Cost So Much?

Soothe2 employs its proprietary algorithm to discern and address exclusively the problematic resonances, preserving the integrity of the remaining audio signal. This approach results in a more harmonious and authentic auditory experience, all while upholding the overall recording quality. The versatility of Soothe2 is striking, applicable across a wide spectrum of audio content such as vocals, guitars, drums, synths, and more. Based on my experience, no other comparable plugin rivals the remarkable sonic clarity and precision inherent in Soothe2's processing.
